To calculate \(15 \times 24\), follow these steps:

### Step 1: Break Down the Multiplication
We'll use the **Distributive Property** to simplify the multiplication:
\[
15 \times 24 = 15 \times (20 + 4) = (15 \times 20) + (15 \times 4)
\]

### Step 2: Perform the Multiplications
- **First Part:** \(15 \times 20\)
  \[
  15 \times 20 = 300
  \]
  
- **Second Part:** \(15 \times 4\)
  \[
  15 \times 4 = 60
  \]

### Step 3: Add the Results Together
\[
300 + 60 = 360
\]

### Final Answer
\[
\boxed{360}
\]
